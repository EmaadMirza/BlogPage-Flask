About Me

This is what I do.

I'm Emaad, a 4th-semester Computer Science Engineering student who got tired of watching tutorials and decided to start building real things instead. My primary focus right now is backend development — Python, Flask, SQLAlchemy, REST APIs, and PostgreSQL — and I grind DSA daily on Striver's A2Z sheet because placements don't wait for anyone.

I've spent the last few months going deep on how the web actually works. I'm talking about building REST APIs, designing database schemas, and implementing authentication from scratch. I wanted to understand why you salt a password before hashing it and exactly what a rainbow table attack looks like. Not surface level. The real stuff.

My next milestone is building an AI News Aggregator — a fully automated pipeline that scrapes YouTube channels and tech blogs, ranks content using an LLM based on my interests, and delivers a personalized digest to my inbox every morning without me touching anything.

After that, the plan is to go deep into cloud architecture and DevOps, then ML and AI engineering, then security. One real project per domain before I decide exactly where to specialize.

This blog is my public record of that journey — what I built, what broke, what I didn't understand and then did. If you're a CS student somewhere between tutorial phases and real projects, hopefully something here is useful to you.
